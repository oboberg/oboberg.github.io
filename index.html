Introduction

I am a postdoc at the University of Washington Department of Astronomy working for the Large Synoptic Survey Telescope (LSST). Most of my time is spent running and analyzing simulations of the 10 year survey that will be undertaken by the LSST starting in 2020. I also work to make the LSST software easily accessible and useable with Docker.

In graduate school I worked with Dr. Eileen Friel and Dr. Enrico Vesperini at Indiana University. In my research, I enjoy exploring what open and globular clusters in the Milky Way can teach us about the history and evolution of our Galaxy and stellar populations, using both observations and computer simulations. I am the co-developer of the Python based pipeline odi-tools used to process and stack images from the One Degree Imager (ODI) on the WIYN 3.5-meter telescope.

I am also very interested in learning and applying data mining and machine learning techniques to research problems in astronomy.

In my free time I enjoy attempting to be a blues guitarist, long distance running, and following Manchester United.
