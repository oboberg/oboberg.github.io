Big Data and Astronomy

While taking classes at IU, I have been introduced to the techniques used in data mining and machine learning through graduate courses in the Computer Science department. Specifically, I took CS-565 (Data Mining) with Dr. Mehmet Dalkilic. Throughout this class I became very interested in applying data mining and machine learning techniques to astronomical data. As we continue to make advancements in astronomy, we will undertake bigger and bigger surveys, such as LSST and GAIA. The amount of data that these surveys, and others, will produce is going to require new sophisticated algorithms to efficiently make sense of it all.

ParaHeap-k (An improved parallel clustering algorithm)

For a final project in my data mining class, I worked with other graduate students, and Dr. Dalkilic, to develop a new and improved version of the k-means algorithm to classify stars in a simulated Milky Way catalog into one of the three Galactic components: thin disk, thick disk, and halo. The work that we did was published in a special issue of the September 2014 IEEE Computer Society journal Computer: Computing in Astronomy.
